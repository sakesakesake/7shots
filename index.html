What is Sake?

Sake is a Japanese rice wine that is made during a brewing process by fermenting rice, during which the starches are converted into sugar, and then alcohol. The alcohol content of sake generally ranges from 14% to 16%, with the exception of the "genshu" variety of sake which has a higher alcohol content of 18% to 20%.

Types of Sake

There are 8 types of sake in Japan. Those types are separated depending on how much amount of rice is used in the process and what are added in sake. There are more detailed categories that sake can be apply to, but it is a bit too much to mention because there are tens of way to make sake.

Taste of Sake

The reason there are various way to make sake is that slight change of process can create different taste and flavor.
"Sweet" and "Dry" for taste, "fruity" and "fresh" are popular way to describe sake taste and flavor.
