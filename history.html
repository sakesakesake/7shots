Sake is a link

It is said that people made sake approximately 2000 years ago when rice cultivation was introduced to Japan. The origin of sake was very sacred and was used for religious reasons. 'Mouth bite liquor' which a kind of rice-based alcohol produced by a process involving human saliva as a fermentation starter. The work of biting was limited to shrine maiden only.
It is still consumed on religious special ocasions in Japan. Sometimes sake is offered at festivals at shrines. Also, sake was essential drink for people for celebration such as wedding, seasonal events and new years. Sake is a link between people and the Japanese Gods.
